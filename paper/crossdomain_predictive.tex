\section{Cross-Domain Confirmation and the Predictive-Information Boundary}
\label{sec:crossdomain}

The feedback-usefulness law $\Delta(D)=\tfrac12 r(D)$ (Thm.~\ref{thm:limit}) was
derived for a symmetric two-state \emph{Markov} capacity. Two questions decide how
far it reaches. First, is it an artifact of the GPU-bursting application, or a law of
acting on a delayed observation that holds across physical domains? Second---the
question the mutual-information reframing of \S\ref{sec:mi} raises---\emph{where} does
the single-lag law give the true value of feedback, and where is it merely a lower
bound that a controller using the delayed \emph{history} can beat? We answer both with
one experiment: a calibrated battery over four physical domains.

\paragraph{Two estimands.}
Given a binary state $x_t\in\{0,1\}$ observed only at age $D$, we compare two policies
by their advantage over the best static (observation-oblivious) action:
\begin{align}
\Delta_{\mathrm{pair}}(D)
  &= \Pr[x_t{=}x_{t-D}]-\tfrac12 \;=\; \tfrac12 r(D),
  \label{eq:dpair}\\
\Delta_{\mathrm{hist}}(D)
  &= \max_{\pi}\ \Pr\!\big[\pi(x_{t-D},\dots,x_{t-D-k+1})=x_t\big]-\tfrac12 .
  \label{eq:dhist}
\end{align}
Equation~\eqref{eq:dpair} is an identity: for \emph{any} symmetric binary state the
value of the single delayed sample equals half the lag-$D$ autocorrelation---this is
the INFOCOM law, evaluated pointwise. Equation~\eqref{eq:dhist} is the value of the
best predictor built from the delayed \emph{window}; it can only exceed
\eqref{eq:dpair} when $x_{t-D}$ is not a sufficient statistic, i.e.\ when the state is
\emph{non-Markov}. The gap $\Delta_{\mathrm{hist}}-\Delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ is exactly
the operational content of predictive information beyond one lag
($I(x_t;\mathcal F_{\le t-D})>I(x_t;x_{t-D})$, \S\ref{sec:mi}).

\paragraph{Battery.}
We instantiate the binary state in four physical domains plus two synthetic controls
(Table~\ref{tab:battery}). Acoustics: LOCATA microphone-array recordings---source
azimuth relative to the array (median-split sector) and source voice activity (VAD,
$100$\,Hz). Geophysics: a broadband seismogram (IU.ANMO, $M_w\,8.3$ Illapel), state =
local energy anomaly. Space weather: GOES X-ray flux, $>1$\,MeV integral protons, and
magnetometer, each a local log-flux anomaly at $1$-min cadence. Bioacoustics:
sperm-whale coda exchanges (Dominica), state = current coda is of the dominant type.
Controls: a two-state Markov chain (where \eqref{eq:dpair} is exact and optimal by
construction) and an i.i.d.\ sequence (where $\Delta=0$).

\paragraph{Estimation and calibration.}
$\Delta_{\mathrm{hist}}$ in \eqref{eq:dhist} is a Bayes lookup on the $k{=}6$-tap
delayed window, evaluated \emph{out of sample} by $5$-fold contiguous block
cross-validation, so no train/test distribution shift can inflate the gap. To decide
whether an observed gap is real predictive information rather than finite-sample noise,
we run a surrogate-data test: for each series we generate $M{=}120$ first-order Markov
surrogates matched to its marginal and lag-1 transition (hence \emph{no} structure
beyond one lag---the null), and recompute the block-CV gap on each. A lag is predictive
only where the real gap exceeds the surrogate $97.5$th percentile; a domain is
predictive only if its maximum gap over lags beats the surrogate max-gap null
(family-wise, max-statistic $p<0.05$) \emph{and} clears a practical effect floor of
$0.03$ (twice the i.i.d.\ null). The two synthetic controls calibrate the test: the
Markov chain gives $p=0.44$ and the i.i.d.\ null $p=0.68$---both correctly
non-predictive---confirming the estimator invents no structure.

\paragraph{Result: a clean Markov/non-Markov boundary.}
Table~\ref{tab:battery} and Fig.~\ref{fig:crossdomain} split the six real states into
two regimes with no overlap. \emph{The law is exact} for the smooth or memoryless
states: source azimuth (a continuously moving talker; gap $0.008$, below the effect
floor) and the whale coda-type sequence (gap $0.046$, $p{=}1.00$)---for these,
$x_{t-D}$ is already a sufficient statistic and the single-lag value
\eqref{eq:dpair} \emph{is} the value of feedback, matching the two-state Markov
control. \emph{The law fails} for every structured signal: speech voice activity,
seismic energy, and all three space-weather channels show gaps of $0.11$--$0.37$ at
$p\le 0.008$. In each, the pairwise autocorrelation decays to zero or negative at the
lags where the history is still strongly predictive---at a $1.5$\,s delay VAD has
$r(D)<0$, so \eqref{eq:dpair} calls feedback worthless, while the delayed history
recovers $\Delta\approx0.17$. This is the random-phase counterexample of
\S\ref{sec:limit} realized in data across three physical domains: autocorrelation is
blind to structure that a history-using controller exploits.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The boundary is exactly the one the mutual-information reframing predicts. Where the
state is Markov, $I(x_t;\mathcal F_{\le t-D})=I(x_t;x_{t-D})$ and the INFOCOM law is
tight; the domain (acoustic, geophysical, bioacoustic) is irrelevant, confirming that
$\Delta(D)=\tfrac12 r(D)$ is a law of delayed observation rather than a property of
GPU clusters. Where the state carries memory---syllabic rhythm, seismic coda,
solar-storm dynamics---the pairwise law is only a lower bound and the operative object
is the predictive information $I(x_t;\mathcal F_{\le t-D})$, upper-bounding the
achievable advantage as in Thm.~\ref{thm:mi-bound}. The practical consequence for a
controller acting on stale sensing is sharp: on a Markov capacity, a memoryless
threshold on the freshest sample is optimal (the INFOCOM controller); on a structured
capacity it leaves the history-carried advantage on the table.

\paragraph{Scope.}
The regime is a property of the \emph{observable}, not only the source: for a slowly
trending signal a global-level state is near-Markov while a local-anomaly state of the
same signal is non-Markov, and both are reported here consistently under the local
definition. We use ground-truth or labeled states so that sensor noise does not
confound the comparison; the value of feedback under \emph{noisy} DOA or detection
(where temporal integration is itself a form of predictive information) is the natural
next step. Datasets and analysis are archived at \path{/archive/infocom_battery}.
