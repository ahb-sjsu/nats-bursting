\paragraph{The law across domains.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:limit} assumes a symmetric two-state \emph{Markov} capacity---is
$\Delta(D)=\tfrac12 r(D)$ special to GPU bursting, or a property of delayed observation?
We tested it on real binary states from other physical domains---acoustic source azimuth
(LOCATA) and sperm-whale coda type (Dominica). On each, the value of the best predictor
from the delayed \emph{history} (out-of-sample block cross-validation) stays on the
single-lag value $\tfrac12 r(D)$ and inside a $95\%$ band of $120$ Markov surrogates
matched on the lag-1 transition (max gaps $0.008$ and $0.046$; controls: synthetic Markov
$p{=}0.44$, i.i.d.\ $\Delta{=}0$): the single-lag law is exact, not a lower bound. The
value of feedback is thus set by the capacity autocorrelation at the sensing lag
independent of physical domain. For a capacity predictable beyond one lag (non-Markov) it
is only a lower bound, the general converse being the $\beta$-mixing bound of
App.~\ref{app:beta}.
